# Budgeting and the 50/30/20 rule

A budget is simply a plan for where your money goes before it disappears. A popular starting framework is the 50/30/20 rule: aim for about 50 percent of after tax income on needs like rent, groceries, transport and bills, 30 percent on wants like eating out and subscriptions, and 20 percent on saving or paying down debt.

The percentages are a guide, not a law. If your rent is high, your needs slice will be bigger and you adjust the rest. The real win is awareness: tracking what you actually spend, spotting subscriptions you forgot, and giving every dollar a job.

This is general information only, not personal financial advice.
