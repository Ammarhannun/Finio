# ETFs (Exchange Traded Funds)

An ETF is a fund that holds many shares or assets at once and trades on the stock exchange like a single share. Buying one ETF gives you instant diversification across hundreds of companies instead of picking individual stocks.

Popular broad options for Australians include funds that track the ASX 200 (largest Australian companies) or global indexes. ETFs usually charge a small yearly management fee called the expense ratio, often well under one percent. Lower fees mean more of the return stays with you.

ETFs suit long term investing because markets tend to rise over many years even though they fall in the short term. They are general information only, not a personal recommendation, and all investing carries risk.
