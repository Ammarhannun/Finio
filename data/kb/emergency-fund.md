# Emergency fund

An emergency fund is cash set aside for surprises like a car repair, a medical bill, or losing your job. It stops you reaching for a credit card or going into debt when life happens.

A common starting target is one thousand dollars, then building toward three to six months of essential expenses. Keep it somewhere safe and easy to reach, such as a separate high interest savings account, not invested in shares where the value can drop right when you need it.

Build it before you start investing, because it is the buffer that lets you invest for the long term without panic selling. This is general information only, not personal financial advice.
